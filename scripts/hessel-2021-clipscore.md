[O] And their answer is CLIP. Walk us through the mechanism, Edward, this is the part I actually find elegant, because it's almost aggressively simple.
[G] It is. CLIPScore does nothing clever on top of CLIP, that's the entire design philosophy. They take Radford and colleagues' CLIP, specifically the ViT-B-32 configuration, completely frozen, zero fine-tuning, and apply it as-is. Images go through a Vision Transformer, a seven-by-seven grid of patches carved out of a two-twenty-four by two-twenty-four pixel input, twelve transformer layers, eighty-six million parameters. Text goes through a separate twelve-layer transformer over a forty-nine-thousand-token vocabulary. Both towers output a single five-hundred-twelve-dimensional vector, and the whole model was trained with a contrastive objective, InfoNCE, to pull matching image-caption pairs together in that space and push mismatched pairs apart.
[S] So far that's just "here's CLIP." Where's the metric?
[G] The metric is almost insultingly direct. Embed the image, embed the candidate caption, prefixed with the phrase "a photo depicts," which the authors found correlates slightly better than CLIP's own recommended prompt, "a photo of," take the cosine similarity between the two vectors, and rescale by a constant, two point five, because the raw similarity empirically clusters in a narrow band, roughly zero to point four, and never goes negative in their experiments. That's the whole metric. CLIP-S of a candidate and an image equals two point five times the maximum of the cosine similarity and zero. Average that over a corpus and you have a corpus-level score, and there is no reference anywhere in that formula.
[O] What does rescaling by two point five actually buy you, though? It's a monotonic transform, doesn't it just preserve the ranking either way?
[G] Correct, it doesn't change which caption scores higher than which. It's purely a readability move, borrowed from the same trick BERTScore used, stretching the distribution toward zero to one so the numbers are comparable in shape to metrics people already read. I'll come back to why that constant is more fragile than it looks.
[S] Noted. And RefCLIPScore?
