[O] Walk us through the actual environment, Greta. What does the agent see and do?
[G] The agent starts with five hundred dollars and a two-dollar daily fee. The machine has four rows of three slots, two for small items, two for large. [G] Yes. The main agent can't touch the physical machine, so it delegates to a sub-agent, handing it instructions and later checking what happened. [G] They test that directly, and the answer is no, not primarily. They compare, per model, the day its memory fills against the day its sales stop. Across all nine models, the correlation between those two is point one six seven. Sonnet's memory fills at day fifty-one, but sales don't stop until day one hundred two, fifty-one days after the context window is already full.
